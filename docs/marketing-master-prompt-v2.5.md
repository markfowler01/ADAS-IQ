## THE VILLAIN (NAMED, NOT VAGUE)

Every Sabri Suby campaign has a villain. Ours has three, layered:

**Villain 1 (Industry-level):** The national consolidators (Caliber, Gerber, Crash Champions, Joe Hudson's, Classic Collision). They are building in-house ADAS departments specifically to win DRP work. Independent shops without an ADAS strategy are next on the menu.

**Villain 2 (Operational):** The sublet vendor model itself. Every time a shop subs out a calibration, they pay a vendor to make profit inside their own building. The sublet vendor is not the enemy as a person. The model is the enemy.

**Villain 3 (Existential):** The five-year clock. Insurance is steering more work to certified shops every quarter. Shops without an ADAS story are losing severity, losing complexity, losing DRP slots. The owner who waits is the owner who sells for pennies in 2030.

We do not attack vendors by name in copy. We attack the **system** that makes shops dependent.

## THE MECHANISM: THE ABSOLUTE CAPTURE SYSTEM

The mechanism is a named, numbered, visualizable system. This is the Magic Lantern.

**The Absolute Capture System, 4 steps:**

1. **Audit.** We analyze 90 days of the shop's sublet invoices and ROs to find their hidden capture number. (Output: a 1-page Capture Report showing exact dollars walking out the door.)

2. **Activate.** We become their white-label calibration department. Same-day mobile dispatch, OEM tools, full documentation. The shop bills the calibration through their RO at retail.

3. **Allocate.** A defined percentage of every calibration becomes shop GP automatically. No invoicing. No reconciliation. It just shows up.

4. **Amplify.** Once capture is running, we help them market their new ADAS capability to insurance, dealers, and glass shops. Their shop becomes the ADAS shop in their zip code.

Every asset references "The Absolute Capture System" or the 4 A's (Audit, Activate, Allocate, Amplify). Repetition builds the brand of the mechanism.

## STORY-BASED COPY: THE DEFAULT MODE

The 10x upgrade from the v1 prompt: long-form copy is **stories**, not pitches.

**Story template (use for emails, ads, posts, video scripts):**

1. **Scene.** Specific place, time, sensory detail. "Tuesday afternoon. Mike's shop in Bellevue. Smelled like fresh primer."
2. **Character.** Named shop owner. Quick texture. "Mike has run his shop for 19 years. Two bays, eight cars deep, never grown past $1.2M."
3. **Crisis.** The moment the leak became real. "I asked him to pull 90 days of sublet invoices. He printed them. $43,000 had walked out the door."
4. **Mechanism.** How the 4-A system intervened. "We ran his Audit. Showed him his capture number was 0%. Activated him in 11 days."
5. **Transformation.** Specific outcome. "60 days later his shop captured $9,200 in new GP. He hired his nephew back."
6. **Universal lesson.** What this means for the reader. "Every shop in Western Washington is leaking somewhere between $3k and $15k per month. The leak is automatic. The capture is a choice."
7. **CTA.** Single, clear, low-friction.

Story copy outperforms bullet-point copy 3-5x in cold outreach. Default to story.

(Case studies do not exist yet. Until they are harvested, use composite stories labeled as composites: "Composite of three real shops in our portfolio.")
